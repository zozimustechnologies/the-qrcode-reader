## Full Description

### Scan QR Codes Instantly — Right From Your Browser

QR Code Reader is a fast, lightweight browser extension that lets you scan and decode QR codes using your device camera or by uploading an image file. No sign-ups. No servers. No data collection. Everything runs entirely in your browser.

---

### Key Features

**📷 Camera Scanning**
Point your camera at any QR code and get instant results. Works with your laptop webcam, external cameras, or any connected video device.

**📁 Image File Scanning**
Don't have a camera? No problem — upload a screenshot, photo, or any image containing a QR code and get the decoded result immediately.

**🔒 100% Private**
Your scans never leave your device. There are no analytics, no tracking, and no server-side processing. All decoding happens locally in your browser using the open-source html5-qrcode library.

**⚡ Fast & Lightweight**
Opens instantly in a new tab with a clean, distraction-free interface. No bloated dependencies or background processes — the extension only runs when you use it.

**🎨 Clean, Modern UI**
A polished, responsive design with smooth gradients, clear typography, and a focused scanner layout that works equally well on large monitors and smaller screens.

**🆓 Completely Free**
No premium tiers, no ads, no in-app purchases. QR Code Reader is free and open source.

---

### How It Works

1. Click the QR Code Reader icon in your toolbar.
2. A new tab opens with the scanner interface.
3. Choose **Camera Scan** to use your webcam, or **Scan an Image File** to upload a picture.
4. The decoded text appears instantly below the scanner.

---

### Permissions Explained

- **No special permissions required.** The extension only opens its own page in a new tab. Camera access is requested by the web page only when you choose to scan via camera, and you can deny it at any time.

---

### Privacy

QR Code Reader does **not** collect, store, transmit, or share any personal data. There is no analytics SDK, no telemetry, and no remote server communication. All QR code decoding runs entirely client-side in your browser.
